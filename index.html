Who’s That?

An aspiring digital product designer based in Berlin. Read a bit more about how I got where I am or jump straight to my projects.

I have a bachelor’s degree in physics from the Freie Universität Berlin. I studied physics mostly to prove to myself that I could do it. Reasearch for my thesis involved beam time at the BESSY II synchrotron radiation source.

This is also where I picked up a lot of useful skills like Python, Java and JavaScript, and how to process and visualise data properly.

Right now, I’m enrolled in a bachelor’s program in communication design at HTW Berlin.

Recently I’ve been working on my JavaScript skills in combination with some fancy UI / UX stuff. I love this kind of work because it exposes me to awesome tools and technology like Figma, Framer, THREE.js and D3.js.

On the side, I am a chairman at Sehen und Ernten.

I also proudly bartend part-time at Alter Roter Löwe Rein. I believe it’s important to know how to make a decent gin fizz and how to correctly pour a beer.
I really enjoy the comradery of working in a great team, but I will say that the hours are pretty terrible.

Some other usefull skills include repairing bicycles, fluent academic English (TOEFL score of 112⁄120) and even a driver’s license!

Portfolio

Over the years I have accumulated large amounts of projects—some better than others. The following is just a glimpse into some of the project that are particularily close to my heart.

Value Driven Graphics

2018 “Berliner Luft” is a companion app concept for indoor air quality sensors.
This was a holistic semester project where students were free to choose their own topic and had to undergo each step of product develepment with an emphasis on dynamic vector graphics.

Collaborator: Philine Schell
Lecturer: Torsten Malcherczyk
Type: Concept | dynamic prototype
Tools: Figma, Framer, THREE.js, D3.js

Mobility as a Service

2017 “cycleto” resulted from an intense 5-day design sprint. The theme was mobility and each team of 2 had to come up with a product, undertake extensive research and visualise their concept under well-defined time constraints.

Collaborator: Louis Tatschke
Lecturer: Matthias Flucke
Type: Concept | Prototype
Tools: Figma, Flinto, After Effects

Technology I

2017 “Techno” was a full-class-effort to build a solid foundation in web technologies by collaborating on an online magazine.
Me and my team built the landing page, as well as an article on a podcast called “Design Details”.

Collaborators: Yannic Scheffel & Miriam Zils
Lecturer: Hans Pagel
Type: 2 Static websites
Tools: Sketch, fullPage.js, GitHub, lots of vanilla JavaScript

Digital Media

2017 “TODO” is the result of my first ever course on digital media.
After little exercises like designing icons, wireframes and information architectures, this was our vision for a to-do iPad app, tailored for K–12 schools.

Collaborators: Joao Wichert & Rebecca Merdes
Lecturer: Andreas Ingerl
Type: UI | prototype
Tools: Sketch, Flinto
