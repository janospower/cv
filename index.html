About Me

I’m a Berlin based designer from Austria with a fairly unique skillset. Here’s a quick vitæ of sorts if you’re interested, but you can also go ahead and jump straight to my selected works below.

I have a bachelor’s degree in physics from the Freie Universität Berlin. I studied physics mostly to prove to myself that I could do it. Reasearch for my thesis involved beam time at the BESSY II synchrotron radiation source.

This is also when I picked up a lot of useful skills like Python and Java, and how to process and visualise data properly.

I also proudly bartend part-time at Alter Roter Löwe Rein. I believe it’s important to know how to make a decent gin fizz and how to correctly pour a beer. I really enjoy the comradery of working in a great team, as well as making sure the customer has a good time.

Currently, I’m enrolled in a bachelor’s program in communication design at HTW Berlin.

I plan to specialise in UI / UX design but am not set on it. I like to try all kinds of disciplines while still within the academic context, and I enjoy a variety of subjects such as photography, typography and 3D- and web-technologies, just to name a few.
On the side, I am a chairman at Sehen und Ernten

Since my childhood years, I’ve been helping my dad with his bike upcycling project REBIKEL be it by designing the logo, a new WordPress site or the interior for a new shop, or just working on the bicycles themselfes.

This way I learned a tremendous amount about all kinds of tools and materials, and got a glimpse at the basics of running an SMB.
